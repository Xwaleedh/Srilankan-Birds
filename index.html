The Birds

Jungle Fowl

Red Junglefowl, wild ancestors of domestic chickens, display vibrant plumage and territorial behavior in South and Southeast Asian habitats. They also hold cultural significance and face habitat threats.

Blue magpie

The Sri Lankan Blue Magpie, endemic to Sri Lanka, boasts stunning blue plumage and a long, elegant tail. It's renowned for its raucous calls and unique, arboreal lifestyle, making it a captivating avian species.

Green Pigeon

The Sri Lankan Green Pigeon, a charming endemic species, showcases a brilliant emerald-green plumage. These frugivorous birds play a crucial role in seed dispersal throughout the island's lush forests, contributing to the ecosystem's health.

Crimson-Backed Flameback

The Sri Lankan Crimson-Backed Flameback, a stunning woodpecker, displays vibrant crimson plumage. It is known for its distinctive drumming sounds as it drills into tree trunks in search of insects, contributing to the symphony of Sri Lanka's forests.

White Throated Kingfisher

The White-Throated Kingfisher is a striking bird found in Asia. With vibrant blue plumage and a distinctive call, it's a skilled hunter, diving from perches to catch fish and insects. It's a symbol of good luck in some cultures.

Black Capped Bul-Bul

The Black-capped Bulbul, native to Southeast Asia, is known for its striking appearance, featuring a black crown and vibrant yellow body. These melodious birds are proficient singers, filling the forests with sweet, varied calls, and they play a vital role in seed dispersal.

Blue Tailed Bee-eater

The Blue-tailed Bee-eater is a captivating bird found in Asia. Its name is derived from its diet of bees and other flying insects. With striking plumage and acrobatic flight, it's a skilled aerial hunter, often seen perched on wires or branches.
